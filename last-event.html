The last “International conference on Earth and Environmental Sciences & Technology for Sustainable Development - 2020” was organized with a view to opening the horizon of blue economy, improving the understanding of geo-resources, understanding the changing climate and disaster risk in a global context, studying urban life and regional development, analyzing and forecasting the atmosphere, and figuring out the sustainable Delta Management. Thousands of earth scientists and students enlightened the event with their spontaneous presence in the conference.

The Event of First Conference

First ICEEST at a glance,
- Held on 25-30 January 2020 at Hotel Intercontinental Dhaka, Bangladesh.
- Chief Guest: The Honorable President of the Peoples Republic of Bangladesh, His Excellency Mr. Abdul Hamid attended as the Chief Guest in the opening ceremony of the conference.
- Inauguration: The Honorable Minister of Education, Dr. Dipu Moni, MP inaugurated the conference program.
- Total 6 subthemes with 24 thematic areas.
- 73 international participant from 20 countries and 580 local researchers attended in the conference.


- Total 32 sessions were arranged to present 465 scientific articles and 800 conference books were printed and gifted to each participant as a conference material.
- A well-organized field trips were conducted in the world’s largest mangrove forest, the Sundarbans area and another field trip was conducted in the world’s longest sea beach, the Cox’s Bazar area.
- 22 national and international organizations and academic institutions came forward with their financial support for the conference.
- Concluding ceremony: Dr. Hasan Mahmud, MP, Honorable Minister, Ministry of Information was presented as Chief Guest. The Faculty of Earth and Environmental Sciences organized the last “International Conference on Earth and Environmental Sciences & Technology for Sustainable Development
- 2020” held on 25-30 January 2020 in Hotel InterContinental Dhaka, Bangladesh. The Faculty of Earth and Environmental Sciences organized the last “International Conference on Earth and Environmental Sciences & Technology for Sustainable Development - 2020” held on 25-30 January 2020 in Hotel InterContinental Dhaka, Bangladesh.The Honorable President of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, His Excellency Mr. Md. Abdul Hamid and the Honorable Minister of Education, Dr. Dipu Moni, MP, graciously attended the opening ceremony of the conference. Moreover, Dr. Muhammad Hasan Mahmud, MP, Honorable Minister, Ministry of Information, was present as the Chief Guest in the closing ceremony of the conference. Considering the fundamental and applied areas of the disciplines as well as addressing cross- cutting issues, six sub-themes including 24 thematic areas were identified for the conference: Blue Economy: Global and Bay of Bengal Perspectives, Geo-Resources Exploration, Development and Management, Disaster Resilience and Sustainability, Environmental Perspectives on Population Dynamics and Regional Development, Meteorology, Weather Forecasting and Application; and Delta Processes and Management. UN Women, Bangladesh joined the conference with another thematic area entitled “Gender Sensitive Infrastructures”. Scientific and technical including financial supports were received from 22 national and international organizations, comprising of academic institutions (viz. University of Dhaka, University College London), relevant public and private agencies, World Bank, NGOs and scientific societies. In the conference, around 73 international participants from 20 countries and 580 local researchers attended. A total of 465 scientific articles were presented in 32 sessions. Eight hundred copies of conference book including 465 abstracts were printed and distributed among the participants including other conference materials.
